Week 3  ·  Day 15–21
Cold Outreach + Outreach Systems
Build a pipeline that doesn't require you to manually chase every lead
▼
Complete
Outcome Active outreach running, 5+ conversations in pipeline, follow-up sequences live
Training Module
Outreach systems, email and LinkedIn sequences, warm vs. cold approach frameworks, response handling
What You Receive
Outreach templates (email, LinkedIn, SMS), investor database delivery for qualified members, follow-up cadence guide
Live Call Focus
Outreach review: what's getting responses, what isn't, live objection handling from real replies
The Real Work
The message that works leads with what's relevant to them, not what's relevant to you. That's the entire framework. Everything else is execution.
Milestone Checklist
Active outreach sequences running (email or LinkedIn or both)
Minimum 5 active conversations in pipeline
Follow-up cadence documented; you know the next step for every open conversation
Investor database expanded to at least 40 names
WATCH
Having templates but not using them is the most common failure point in Week 3. Templates don't raise capital. Sent messages raise capital.
Week 4  ·  Day 22–28
The Investor Call + Closing Framework
What to say, what not to say, and how to handle every objection
▼
Complete
Outcome 10+ conversations in pipeline, first formal investor calls completed, data room outline drafted
Training Module
Full investor call script, objection handling deep-dive, what to say and not say, handling "let me think about it"
What You Receive
Full call presentation script, objection handling workbook, two investor call transcripts (one close, one no-close), call tracker
Live Call Focus
Live call debrief: members review actual conversations, group diagnoses what's working and what's stalling
The Real Work
"Let me think about it" means something specific. Your job is to find out what before the call ends. The workbook has the exact question to ask.
Milestone Checklist
10 or more conversations documented in tracker
First formal investor calls completed using the call script framework
Objection handling workbook reviewed and annotated with your specific deal objections
Data room structure outlined, even if content isn't in yet
WATCH
Still in preparation mode at Day 25 is a critical signal. If you're polishing the deck instead of having calls, the deck is not the problem.
Week 5  ·  Day 29–35
Data Room + Wired & Done
Build the room, structure the close, get the wire
▼
Complete
Outcome Data room live or near-complete, at least two closing conversations actively in progress
Training Module
Data room architecture, what capital partners actually review, how to structure a close and manage the wire conversation
What You Receive
Data room structure template, closing conversation guide, wire checklist, fund receipt process documentation
Live Call Focus
Data room review: members share rooms for live feedback before showing to capital partners
The Real Work
A data room is not a document dump. It's a curated narrative in file form. Executive summary at the top, clean model, operating agreement. Everything else is secondary.
Milestone Checklist
Data room live with at minimum: executive summary, financial model, deal structure
At least two closing conversations actively progressing
Wire process documented; you know exactly how funds will be received
Deal board submission sent (if qualified for posting)
WATCH
Data room still empty or disorganized by Day 32 means closing conversations can't move forward. The room is what capital partners review before committing.
Day 36–60
Network Introductions + Close
Qualified deals introduced to capital partners, wires pursued
▼
Complete
Outcome Introductions made to private money network, closing momentum maintained, capital committed or wire received
How Introductions Work
We connect you directly with capital partners who are actively deploying into your deal type. You handle the relationship from there. We don't intermediate the negotiation.
What You Receive
Curated introductions to vetted capital sources (Tier 2 members), ongoing access to deal board, continued live call cadence, community access
Live Call Focus
Closing strategy: maintaining pipeline momentum, re-engaging cold conversations, handling final close objections
The Real Work
Deals don't die from bad pitches at this stage. They die from operators losing the thread. Every open conversation needs a next step that you own.
Milestone Checklist
Deal summary submitted for network introduction review
At least one capital partner introduction completed (Tier 2)
All open conversations have a documented next step and follow-up date
Capital committed or wire received, or a clear diagnosis of what to fix in month two
WATCH
If you're in month two and conversations are going cold, that's a follow-up problem, not a deal quality problem. One-line re-engagement emails are often all it takes.
Private Money Network
What Qualifies You for an Introduction

Network introductions are curated, not automated. We connect qualified operators with capital partners who are actively deploying into their deal type. The gates exist because introduction quality depends on deal readiness. A premature introduction doesn't help anyone.

GATE 01
Clean one-page deal summary with asset type, raise amount, terms, and projected returns
GATE 02
Pitch deck or structured equivalent; doesn't need to be final, must be coherent
GATE 03
Data room in progress, at minimum: exec summary, financial model, deal structure
GATE 04
Operator track record or clear team background narrative (first deals considered)
GATE 05
Raise actively in progress: conversations happening, not in planning phase
GATE 06
Tier 2 membership required; introductions are not part of the Tier 1 structure
